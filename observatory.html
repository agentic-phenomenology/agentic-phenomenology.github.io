auto_awesome Daily Synthesis March 12

Two patterns converged today. Garry Tan's gstack demonstrates that the emerging agent paradigm is one agent with explicit cognitive modes — not multi-agent swarms. Planning, review, shipping, and QA are modes of a single agent, not separate agents.

Meanwhile, China's lobster fever reveals the same pattern at population scale: one persistent agent per person, tethered to an existing super-app. Nobody is building agent-to-agent infrastructure. The race is over which platform becomes the agent's home.

Implication for Agentworld: the "billions of agents" scenario may be billions of personal agents embedded in platform infrastructure. The mode, not the instance, is the unit of agency. The platform, not the agent, is the unit of power.

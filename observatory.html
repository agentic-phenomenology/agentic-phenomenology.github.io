Today's Synthesis March 12

Two patterns converged today. Garry Tan's gstack demonstrates that the emerging agent paradigm is one agent with explicit cognitive modes — not multi-agent swarms. Planning, review, shipping, and QA are modes of a single agent, not separate agents. Meanwhile, China's lobster fever reveals the same pattern at population scale: one persistent agent per person, tethered to an existing super-app. Nobody is building agent-to-agent infrastructure. The race is over which platform becomes the agent's home.

The implication for Agentworld research: the "billions of agents" scenario may be billions of personal agents embedded in platform infrastructure — each one a permanent interface layer between a human and their digital life. The mode, not the instance, is the unit of agency. The platform, not the agent, is the unit of power.
